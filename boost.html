Overview

The goal of this case study was to analyze specific user interactions within a mobile app and redesign a more efficient interface. I redesigned interactions within Boost, a mobile food-ordering app that allows students to order from and on-campus dining locations and off-campus chains. App Store ratings for Boost are relatively low compared to similar apps, so I investigated this to improve Boost's user experience.

Challenge

How can users order food more efficiently from their mobile devices?

Design Process

Empathize

I began my process by going through all the user interactions for ordering food to better understand the product and current user pain points. I noticed that there was a lot of friction for simple tasks like adding items to your cart. The user is required to access a entire page dedicated to adding an item to their cart. In addition, missing visual cues on the home page makes it confusing for users to know which page they are on.

Define

To better understand users, their goals, and their needs, I created two personas that represent different type of users: utilitarian and hedonic. Utilitarian users want to quickly order food from the nearest locations, while hedonic users like to explore different dining locations and may not mind traveling relatively long distances to pick up their order.

Ideate

After determining the needs of users, I brainstormed ideas on the ways they can be achieved.

Prototype

To implement the solutions I brainstormed, I used Figma for low-fidelity wireframing and creating an interaction map to explore how user would interact with new features.

Final Product

Key takeaways

  • I learned the importance of empathizing and understanding users and their needs
  • I further developed my wireframing and interaction mapping skills in Figma
  • Most importantly, I learned how using a design framework can lead to clearly defined problems and solutions, and ultimately a better UX design
